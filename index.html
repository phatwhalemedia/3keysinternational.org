Myers Briggs Type Indicator® The Enneagram
This tool determines how we are energized, take in information and make decisions. Rational, conscious thinking is defined by a person's MBTI®, it also provides a window into the person's worldview. Decisions made when rational thinking is in control are always in the best interest of the person, as opposed to the subconscious emotional defense. The Enneagram is best understood as the defense system that is located in the subconscious. It is best determined by referencing a stressful situation and examining emotions and motivations rather than behaviours. There are nine different Enneagram types, each identified by a number. This defense is fully subconscious and not located in rational thinking. Therefore, decisions made with the Enneagram in control are made from a position of emotional defense and not from rational thinking.
